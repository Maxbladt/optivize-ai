# Optivaize.nl Content Quality & E-E-A-T Audit
**Date:** 2026-03-27
**Auditor:** Content Quality Specialist (Google Sept 2025 QRG Framework)
**Site:** https://optivaize.nl (AI agency, De Bilt, Netherlands)

---

## Executive Summary

| Metric | Score |
|--------|-------|
| **Overall Content Quality** | **62/100** |
| E-E-A-T Composite | 59/100 |
| AI Citation Readiness | 48/100 |
| Technical SEO Foundations | 78/100 |

The site has solid technical SEO foundations (structured data, canonical tags, meta descriptions) and a well-designed UI. However, it suffers from **thin content on most pages**, **weak first-hand experience signals**, **missing author bios and credentials**, and **client-side rendering that hides blog content from crawlers**. The homepage is the strongest page, while the Blog listing, Cases, and Contact pages are critically thin.

### 4. Cases (https://optivaize.nl/cases)
**Content Quality Score: 38/100 -- CRITICAL**

| Element | Finding | Rating |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Title tag | "Cases & Succesverhalen \| Optivaize, AI-bureau De Bilt" (55 chars) | Good |
| Meta description | "Bekijk onze AI cases en succesverhalen. Ontdek hoe Optivaize bedrijven heeft geholpen met AI automatisering, marketing en software vanuit De Bilt." (146 chars) | Good |
| H1 | "Cases & Resultaten" | Acceptable |
| Word count | ~311 words | CRITICALLY THIN |
| Canonical | https://optivaize.nl/cases | Correct |

**Heading Hierarchy:**
- H1: 1, H2: 1 ("Succesverhalen"), H3: 0. Extremely sparse. The cases themselves are loaded dynamically via API call.

**E-E-A-T Signals:**
- Experience (5/20): Cases are loaded via API and rendered client-side. Googlebot may see only the hero section with ~311 words. The actual case content is likely invisible to crawlers.
- Expertise (6/25): No visible expertise in the static HTML.
- Authoritativeness (8/25): The page references client names in its description but the body is hollow in SSR output.
- Trustworthiness (14/30): Breadcrumb schema present.

**AI Citation Readiness:** 20/100
- Almost nothing quotable in the server-rendered HTML.

**This is the most critical issue on the site.** The cases page is a cornerstone E-E-A-T signal for an agency. Dynamic client-side loading means Google may never index the actual case studies.

**Recommendations:**
1. **URGENT:** Server-side render (SSR) all case studies. Pre-fetch cases in `generateMetadata` or use `getServerSideProps` equivalent in Next.js App Router.
2. Add introductory content (300-500 words) describing your approach, methodology, and types of projects.
3. Each case card should include a visible 1-sentence summary, client name, and key metric in the SSR HTML.
4. Add aggregate statistics (e.g., "X cases afgerond, gemiddeld Y% verbetering").

3. Add Article schema (JSON-LD) with author, datePublished, dateModified.
4. Ensure H1, H2, H3 heading structure is present in SSR output.

## AI Content Quality Assessment (Sept 2025 QRG)

### Risk Flags for AI-Generated Content
The following pages show patterns consistent with AI-generated content that lacks sufficient human editorial oversight:

1. **Service pages (AI Agenten, AI Marketing):** The phrasing is polished but somewhat generic. Sentences like "Van nul naar volledig AI-marketing in 5 stappen" follow templated patterns. The content reads well but lacks specific anecdotes, failed experiments, nuanced opinions, or contrarian viewpoints that signal authentic expertise.

2. **Homepage identity section:** "Geen hype. Geen powerpoints." is a good differentiator, but the supporting text follows predictable AI-generated structure (problem, solution, benefit pattern without variation).

**Recommendation:** Add first-person narratives, specific project challenges and how they were solved, and opinions on industry trends that could only come from direct experience.
